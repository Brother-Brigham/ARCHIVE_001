## 19β Story
![[IMG_3724.jpeg]]![[IMG_3725.jpeg]]
And when it was becoming evening, they met a certain shepherd who was driving his sheep down the road. And he, having seen them approaching, having fallen into fear, he stopped and, having shouted at them, said, "Who are you, that travel through the night? From where did you come, and where are you going?" And Dicaeopolis approaching, explained all that happened, and the shepherd, having kindly received them, said, "But all are under the protection of Zeus, both beggars and strangers. But it is already becoming night, I advise you not to spend the night alone in the hills. Come now, come with me to my hut, where you may stay the night." And so they gladly received the shepherd's words and followed him to a certain small hut. And the shepherd said, "Look--go in. On the one hand I will milk both the goats and the sheep, and on the other hand you put down your baggage, light a fire, and sit down."
![[IMG_3726.jpeg]]
On the one hand Philip lit a fire, and on the other his father sat down and rested from the long journey. And the shepherd, having milked the sheep, returned and prepared dinner, both bread and cheese and milk. And Dicaeopolis said, "Look, friend; a certain hunter, whom we met down the road, gave to us this hare. Do you wish to roast it for dinner?" And he said, "Certainly; for thus we will eat most pleasantly; and after dinner, the bot will sing songs." Then they roasted the hare and ate pleasantly; and on the one hand Philip sang songs, and on the other hand the shepherd told stories, until all were so tired that they fell into a deep sleep.

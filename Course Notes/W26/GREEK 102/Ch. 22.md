## 22a Story
![[Pasted image 20260309122123.png]]
And the assembly coming to an end and the citizens leaving, Dicaeopolis said, "Come then, boy, let us hurry homeward so that we may explain all of the things that have happened to your mother." So they began to journey most quickly and, having become night, they arrived into the house. And when Dicaeopolis had knocked on the door, Myrrhine came out and, having seen Philip healthy and seeing, embraced him and rejoicing, began to weep. And when they had come in and both washed and eaten, on the one hand Philip explained all that had happened on the journey and at the sanctuary of Asclepius; and she was enjoying listening.

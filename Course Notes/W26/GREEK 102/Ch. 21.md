And not much later Dicaeopolis, having risen, said to Philip, "Stand up, boy, for it is time to go. So let us hurry straight toward the city." So having set out, after a short time they began to meet many farmers going to Athens. So Dicaeopolis, having approached some old man who was walking near him, began to ask for what reason so many hurry to Athens. And he said, “What are you saying, man? Do you not know this, that today there will be an assembly? Therefore for that reason we all hurry toward the city, so that in the assembly we may hear the speakers. For the greatest matters lie before the people, about which it is necessary to deliberate.” And Dicaeopolis said, “But what exactly lies before the people, old man?” And he said, “But who does not know this, that it is necessary to deliberate whether we should make war to the Peloponnesia

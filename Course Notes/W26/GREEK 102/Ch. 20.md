## 20γ Story
![[IMG_3754.jpeg]]![[IMG_3755.jpeg]]
And when it became day, having said goodbye to the shepherd, they began to journey and finally they arrived into the top of the hills, from which they looked down seeing both the plain lying below and certain walls standing on the crest of a hill. And Phillip, stopping his father, said, "Papa, I see certain large walls standing up on the crest of that hill. But tell me, what is it?" And Dicaeopolis, looking at the walls for some long time, said, "Those are, child, it seems to me, the walls of Mycenae." And Phillip said, "Do you speak truth? Was that where Agamemnon lived? Do you plan to show me the palace of Agamemnon? Is it possible for us to go down to that place and to see the palace?" And Dicaeopolis said, "Is it possible to go down, if you want. For the walls are not very far from the road, and--for it is late--we'll stay the night safely within the walls."
![[IMG_3756.jpeg]]
Having spoken thus, he led the boy down the hill. Then after a little while they were approaching the walls and, having gone up onto the hill, they arrived at the gates. And Phillip, seeing the walls was amazed at the size and said, "O father, surely giants built these walls; for men would not be able to lift such great stones." And Dicaeopolis said, "You speak truth, child, for the Cyclopes, as they say, made these things. But look, I will show you wonderful work; look up!" And Phillip, looking up, saw two stone lions guarding the gates. And having looked at these they began to move forward and having arrived in the top of the crest of a hill in the palace of Agamemnon itself they began to stop, looking down at both the plain and the sun shining on the sea.
![[IMG_3757.jpeg]]![[IMG_3758.jpeg]]
And suddenly Philip shuddered and fell into an icy fear. “O father”, he said, “this place does not please me. For it smells of blood.” And Dicaeopolis said, “fear nothing, child, maybe the Furies of Agamemnon and the completely evil woman still wander about even now. But they will not harm you, child. Come. Give me your hand. I will lead you.” And having spoken thus, he led the boy down most quickly.

## 20γ Story (Last two paragraphs)
![[IMG_3773.jpeg]]
Then, approaching a certain man who was passing through the road, they asked where an inn was. And he, having looked terribly and having fallen into anger, said, "By the gods! You seem to be Athenians! What do you want? Indeed, what are you doing in Corinth?" And, having shouted to those who are present, he said, "Come here, you all! Friends, these are some Athenians; surely they are spies, who cam to spy on the docks." And Dicaeopolis said, "What are you saying, man? We are not spies, but farmers, who are going from Epidaurus to Athens." But a crowd of Corinthians already gathered, who were wildly shouting; and some also were taking and throwing stones at them.
![[IMG_3774.jpeg]]
So Dicaeopolis, having fallen into a state of fear, said, "Flee, Philip, as fast as you can!" So on the one hand they fled toward the gates, and on the other hand the Corinthians pursuing were throwing stones. And running, both Philip and his father fled and escaped notice, having hidden themselves in a certain ditch, they were staying all the night long. And when it became day they set out immediately, and escaped the notice of all men, going quickly. And when they approached Megara, they did not enter into the city but passed along outside the walls. So thus they escaped notice, having finally entered into Attica and when they first arrived into Eleusis they began to rest, lying by the road; for having suffered many and terrible things, they were tired, so that they were not able to go forward.

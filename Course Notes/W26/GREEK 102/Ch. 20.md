And when it became day, having said goodbye to the shepherd, they began to journey and finally they arrived into the top of the mountains, from which they looked down seeing both the plain lying below and certain walls standing on the crest of a hill. And Phillip, stopping his father, said, "Papa, I see certain large walls standing up on the crest of that hill. But tell me, what is it?" And Dicaeopolis, looking at the walls for some long time, said, "Those are, child, it seems to me, the walls of Mycenae." And Phillip said, "Do you speak truth? Was that where Agamemnon lived? Do you plan to show me the palace of Agamemnon? Is it possible for us to go down to that place and to see the palace?" And Dicaeopolis said, "Is it possible to go down, if you want. For the walls are not very far from the road, and--for it is late--we'll stay the night safely within the walls."

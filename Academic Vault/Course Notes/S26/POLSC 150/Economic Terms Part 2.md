1. Economic System Spectrum: Market Economy v. Command Economy: How much influence does the gov't have in the economic system
The Canadian economy 
2. Free Market/Laissez Faire (system): the gov't has little to no influence in the economy

3. Capitalism (system): an economic system that focuses heavily on creating wealth/making a profit. Interestingly, not limited to free-market systems.

4. Socialism (system): an economic system where the gov't taxes and spends heavily. Can still be a free-market system. 

5. Communism (system): where the gov't owns/controls the entire system. 

6. Monopoly: when one business controls the entire market for one good/service. 

7. Central Bank: a national bank whose primary task is to manage the monetary system of a country (i.e. how much currency is worth). 

8. Inflation: the price of goods and services goes up.

9. Tariff: an additional tax on goods or services purchased from another country (officially paid by the country, but often just paid for by consumers)

10. Quota: when the gov't limits the number of goods or services that can be imported from another country. 

11. Subsidies: when the gov't pays certain industries so they can sell their goods or services for less. 

12. Public Goods: gov't spending that benefits everyone

13. Social Expenditure: gov't spending that benefits people that meet certain criteria (usually people in lower socio-economic categories)

14. Freedom (economic): the right to do what ever we want

15. Equality (economic): everyone gets the same of something, usually an outcome or an opportunity.

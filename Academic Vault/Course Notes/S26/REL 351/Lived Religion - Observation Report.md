### Step 1 — Choose and Observe

For this assignment, I selected the Ebenezer Baptist service on May 10. The service was about two hours long, so I watched about the first hour.

---

### Step 2 — Observe and Record (Description Only)

• What happens first, next, and last  
The service opened with music from the choir, singing a birthday song for everybody whose birthday was in May. They invited everyone to stand and move or clap. Then everybody was invited to stand by the senior pastor, who gave a sermon on motherhood and the subject of mother's day. Then they read a mother's day litany; the pastor read a line, then the congregation read a line, and they repeated that sequence until the litany ended. Then another musical number, this time combined "praise team" and congregation. They transitioned from a more traditional musical number into a more upbeat, high-energy gospel tune with both the praise team and congregation. Another pastor got up and had a highly mother-focused celebratory message, inviting the mothers to stand if able. He highlighted from the daughters to the great-great-grandmothers, talking directly to a couple of 100-year-old women in the congregation. He celebrated a couple of "Mother's Day Honorees" to highlight the example of mothers generally. A female minister also acknowledged some of these honorees. A prayer was offered by the pastor. Another woman came forward and began praying for specific individuals. After the prayers, they brought up several babies to the front of the room to have them blessed and prayed over by the pastors. Following the baby blessings, a few political races in the area were spoken about by the pastor, who advocated for the members of their church who were running. Alexandria Ocasio-Cortez was also there, and was invited to speak for a bit. 
• What is repeated  
Phrases about the greatness of God are repeated constantly, perhaps more than anything else. 
• Who speaks and who acts  
Pastors, the "praise team" (music), the congregation.
• What objects are used  
Microphones, instruments, projector screens, paper note cards.
• What the space looks like  
Looks like a concert hall and a chapel blended; typical rostrum and choir seating and pews, with an additional mezzanine up above the back end of the pews. Also features space on the rostrum for the band.
• How people participate
Lots of standing and clapping and movement during the music.

### Step 4 — Apply the Lenses of Lived Religion

Use the following lenses:

• **Space** — layout, focal points, sacred areas  
The layout of the space is designed to direct attention to the rostrum, leading to a sole focus on whatever's going on there. I'm not sure there were any specifically designated sacred areas.
• **Time** — sequence, rhythm, repetition  
They transitioned very fluidly between portions of the service; there were almost no gaps in time between components. The goodness/greatness/glory of God were emphasized repeatedly.
• **Body** — posture, gesture, movement, stillness  
Posture was either relaxed and seated, or standing with hands upraised or clapping. Especially during the music, movement was emphasized. During the prayer, there was much more stillness.
• **Food** — use or absence of food/drink  
Where seemed to be an absence of food and drink.
• **Text / Authority** — who speaks, leads, or interprets
The Bible and the pastors seemed to be the primary authorities. The Bible functioned as the primary authoritative text, with the pastors leading the congregation and interpreting the text.

### Step 5 — Write Your Analysis (Main Assignment)

Write a short analysis (600–900 words) that explains:

• What was most emphasized in the practice  
• How the practice reflects:

- the problem
- the solution
- the path  
    • How meaning is expressed through space, time, body, or ritual

Focus on how religion is _performed_, not just what it teaches

In the practice of the hour-long portion of the Ebenezer Baptist service that I observed, the problem, solution, and path are most clearly encapsulated in the most-repeated aspect of the service: an explicit call to the goodness, greatness, and glory of God. Though this service in particular spent a significant amount of time focusing on the power of mothers and their importance in the lives of those they mother, even that focus was tied into God's power and influence. It wasn't quite explicitly stated during the first hour of the service, but it was clear that the underlying problem of humanity's powerlessness and corrupt nature was the thought behind these calls out to God for his power to save and make good of the mortal believers.

### Step 6 — Final Reflection

Write 4–6 sentences:

• What surprised or confused you?  
• How did this observation change your understanding of religion?

What surprised me the most were the birthday song at the beginning of the service, as well as the political focus that came to play about halfway through the service, between the admonition to be politically active and to support members of the congregation who were running for political office. The birthday song was just outright unexpected, but the political focus made me think of the relationship between church and state in more depth. After thinking on it, it makes sense to me to have a church leader advocate for certain political participation in order to motivate a large audience to participate in that way; churches are some of the last social groups in the US today with that kind of power to motivate participation, and they are generally very effective given that the membership of churches tend to hold similar beliefs to other members of their religious community. If you can get the whole body of the church to act in favour of its values, you have the potential to enact great social change.

### Step 1 — Choose and Observe

For this assignment, I selected the Ebenezer Baptist service on May 10. The service was about two hours long.

---

### Step 2 — Observe and Record (Description Only)

• What happens first, next, and last  
The service opened with music from the choir, singing a birthday song for everybody whose birthday was in May. They invited everyone to stand and move or clap. Then everybody was invited to stand by the pastor, who gave a sermon on motherhood and the subject of mother's day. Then they read a mother's day litany; the pastor read a line, then the congregation read a line, and they repeated that sequence until the litany ended. Then another musical number, this time combined "praise team" and congregation. They transitioned from a more traditional musical number into a more upbeat, high-energy gospel tune with both the praise team and congregation. Another pastor got up and had a highly mother-focused celebratory message, inviting the mothers to stand if able. He highlighted from the daughters to the great-great-grandmothers, talking directly to a couple of 100-year-old women in the congregation. He celebrated a couple of "Mother's Day Honorees" to highlight the example of mothers generally
• What is repeated  
Phrases about the greatness of God are repeated constantly, perhaps more than anything else. 
• Who speaks and who acts  
Pastors, the "praise team" (music), the congregation,

• What the space looks like  
Looks like a concert hall and a chapel blended; typical rostrum and choir seating and pews, with an additional mezzanine up above the back end of the pews. Also features space on the rostrum for the band.
• How people participate
Lots of standing and clapping and movement during the music.
